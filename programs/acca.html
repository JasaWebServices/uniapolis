About ACCA Qualification

ACCA (the Association of Chartered Certified Accountants) is the global body for professional Accountants. ACCA aims to offer business-relevant, targeted combination of study and practical experience to people of application, ability, and ambition around the world who seek a rewarding career in Accounting, finance, and management. ACCA graduates will be eligible to apply for ISCA membership and ACCA membership with ACCA (UK) upon completion of the requisite work experience.

Entry Requirement

PROFESSIONAL EXAMINATION ROUTE (PER)

  • 18 year old and above
  • Obtained at least 2 credits in GCE A Level and 2 credits in GCE O Levels or completed 3 LCCI Level 3 and 2 LCCI Level 2 relevant courses with a GCE O level credit (C6) in Mathematics
  • Language proficiency is IELTS 4.5 or equivalent

Student Registration

All students wish to sit for exams must register as a member with ACCA/ICPAS.
It normally takes about 3 months to process an application once ACCA/ICPAS have receive your registration form and documents

Examination & Exemption

ACCA offers exemptions for those who have a relevant degree or diploma or other professional qualifications.
All exemptions are subject to final approval by ACCA (UK). ACCA examinations are held twice a year – June and December. Students are allowed to sit for a maximum of 4 papers at each examination sitting. A maximum of 7 years to complete all professional level examination papers is stipulated.
In addition, students need to gain three years’ relevant and supervised practical work experience. These three years can be obtained before, during or after examinations.

Certificate Award

To complete the ACCA Qualification, students need to

  • Pass 14 papers, unless exemption awarded
  • Complete the online Professional Ethics module

ACCA issues exam certificates twice a year after the exam results are released, as follows:

Certificate of Achievement

For those who obtained a pass mark of 85% or above for individual passes in F1, F2 and F3

Fundamental Level Certificate

For those who have successfully completed all of the exams in the Fundamental Level exam (Paper F1-F9) of the ACCA Qualification.

Professional Level Certificate

For those who have successfully completed all of the exams in the Professional Level of the ACCA Qualification (2 essential papers and any 2 of the optional papers).
